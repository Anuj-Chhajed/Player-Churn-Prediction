# Gaming Retention Strategies Knowledge Base

## In-Game Purchases and Monetization
* Zero Purchase Churners: For players who show high playtime but zero purchases, do not aggressively push monetization. Instead, offer a high-value "First Time Buyer" starter pack at a heavy discount (e.g., 90% off for items worth $10). Give them free premium currency during special events to show them the value of premium items.
* Whale Retention: For players who spend heavily but are showing declining session frequency, assign them a dedicated VIP account manager. Offer them exclusive cosmetics that cannot be bought by regular users, only earned through VIP status.

## Session Frequency and Playtime
* Declining Session Frequency: If a player goes from 5 sessions a week to 1 or 2, they are likely experiencing burnout or lack of clear short-term goals. Implement a "Welcome Back" daily login bonus that scales exponentially over 7 days. Give them rested XP (bonus experience points) so they feel they can catch up quickly despite playing less.
* Low Average Session Duration: If sessions are less than 15 minutes, the player is likely unable to commit to long matches. Introduce quick-play modes, auto-resolve mechanics for tedious farming, and ensure there are meaningful micro-progression rewards they can earn within a 10-minute window.

## Progression and Difficulty
* High Difficulty Drop-off: If a player is stuck on a specific level or boss and stops playing, implement dynamic difficulty adjustment (DDA). Secretly lower the boss's health or damage by 10% after 3 consecutive failures. Send an in-game mail offering free consumable buffs (like health potions or extra lives).
* Content Exhaustion: When high-level players run out of achievements to unlock, they churn. Implement procedural endgame activities, infinite scaling leaderboards, and prestige systems that allow them to reset their progress for exclusive permanent profile borders or titles.

## Game Genre Specifics
* Action/FPS Games: Focus on competitive battle passes, limited-time exclusive weapon skins, and community tournaments. Matchmaking should occasionally place them in slightly easier lobbies if they are on a losing streak to maintain engagement.
* RPG Games: Focus on deep lore expansions, daily faction quests, and rare pet drops. Emphasize social guild mechanics, as RPG players are highly retained by social obligations to their guildmates.
